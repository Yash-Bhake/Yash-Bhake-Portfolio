About Me

Bridging Technology and Creative Expression

I'm a fourth-year student at IIT Bombay, majoring in Mechanical Engineering with a Minor in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science. I’ve always been fascinated by the possibilities that emerging technologies bring. My curiosity deepened when I came across the problem of generating aesthetics using AI—it truly captured my imagination. I’m also an ardent fan of music, especially classical art forms, and my goal is to bring these traditions into the computational landscape by exploring ways to understand and model them for deeper research and creative generative applications.

With publications in conferences like ISMIR and ICASSP, I've contributed to the field of Music Information Retrieval through innovative approaches to generative modeling and expressive timing analysis.

I am a creative problem solver with a strong commitment to making a positive impact. I resonate with design principles and bring a mindset focused on iterative innovation and collaboration. I'm Always eager to connect with people who share a passion for bringing technology to unexplored domains, engineering and music. Excited to connect, whether it’s opportunities, ideas, or meaningful conversations.

Publications

Research in Music Information Retrieval and AI

Melodic And Metrical Elements of Expressiveness in Hindustani Vocal Music

Y. Bhake, A. Anand and P. Rao | ISMIR 2025, Daejeon, Korea

Research focused on generative modeling in expressive classical music, exploring computational approaches to capture and model melodic and metrical expressiveness in Hindustani vocal traditions.

Generative Modeling Music Information Retrieval View on ArXiv

Expressive Timing in Hindustani Vocal Music

Y. Bhake and P. Rao | WIMAGA, ICASSP 2025, Hyderabad

Investigation into quantitative methods for capturing expressive timing in classical Indian music, with applications to computational musicology and generative modeling of musical syncopation.
